What I Do

Quality issues on major projects are rarely caused by lack of procedures. They happen when systems don't translate into execution.

My work focuses on closing that gap.

Designing quality systems that reduce NCR recurrence — not just track it
Structuring submittal and approval workflows to improve decision speed and clarity
Using trend data and root cause analysis to eliminate recurring failures at source
Building KPI frameworks that reflect actual project performance — not just reporting metrics
Aligning contractors, consultants, and PMC teams under a unified quality system

My Approach

Most projects treat quality as a control function. I approach it as a system.

That means:

Looking beyond individual NCRs to identify patterns and systemic gaps
Shifting from reactive corrections to preventive control
Creating visibility through structured, decision-focused reporting
Ensuring quality processes support delivery — not slow it down

Most quality systems don't fail because they are incomplete. They fail because they are not used the way they were designed.
